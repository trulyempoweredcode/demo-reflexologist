Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about reflexology and what to expect when you visit. If your question isn't here, please get in touch.

Reflexology is a complementary therapy based on the principle that specific points on the feet correspond to different organs, glands, and systems throughout the body. By applying gentle, targeted pressure to these reflex points, I help stimulate your body's natural healing processes, improve circulation, and promote deep relaxation. It's completely non-invasive and suitable for most people.

Reflexology should never hurt. My technique is firm but gentle — you might feel some areas of tenderness, which can indicate where your body needs attention, but the overall experience is deeply relaxing. Most clients find it so soothing that they drift off during treatment. I always check in with you and adjust the pressure to your comfort level.

Your first appointment lasts around 75 minutes. We'll start with a conversation about your health history, lifestyle, and what you'd like to achieve. I'll then gently examine your feet before beginning the treatment. You'll be seated in a comfortable reclining chair — fully clothed, just with bare feet. Afterwards, I'll share my findings and suggest a treatment plan.

This depends on what you're looking to address. Some clients notice benefits after a single session, while chronic conditions typically respond best to a course of 4–6 weekly treatments. After that, many people choose to come monthly for maintenance. I'll recommend a plan during your initial consultation, but there's never any obligation to commit.

Reflexology can help with common pregnancy discomforts like nausea, back pain, swollen ankles, and fatigue. Reflexology is used to support a wide range of conditions including stress, anxiety, insomnia, chronic pain, migraines, digestive issues, hormonal imbalances, and fertility challenges. While I don't diagnose or claim to cure specific conditions, many clients experience significant improvements in their symptoms. Reflexology works alongside conventional medicine — never as a replacement for it.

Reflexology is safe for most people, but there are a few situations where treatment may need to be modified or delayed — for example, if you have a foot injury, deep vein thrombosis, or are in the first trimester of pregnancy. I'll go through a thorough health questionnaire before your first treatment to make sure everything is safe. If you're unsure, feel free to call me for a quick chat before booking.
